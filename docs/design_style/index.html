Swiss Style:
The International
Typographic Style

Swiss Design, also known as the International Typographic Style, is a graphic design movement that emphasizes clarity, objectivity, and readability. It uses structured grid systems and sans-serif typography to communicate information in a precise and universal way.

History

Swiss Design emerged in Switzerland during the 1950s, influenced by earlier modernist movements such as Bauhaus and De Stijl. Designers like Josef Müller-Brockmann sought to remove unnecessary decoration and focus instead on rational organization and clear communication.

The style became internationally influential due to its effectiveness in signage, editorial design, and corporate identity systems.

Core Principles

  • Grid-based layout systems
  • Sans-serif typography (such as Helvetica)
  • Asymmetrical compositions
  • Minimal ornamentation
  • Clear visual hierarchy
