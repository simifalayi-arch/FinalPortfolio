Swiss Style:
The International
Typographic Style

Swiss Design is a modernist design movement focused on clarity, objectivity, and structure. It relies on grid systems, sans-serif typography, and disciplined layout to communicate information efficiently and universally.

History Principles Demonstration Sources

History

Swiss Design emerged in Switzerland during the 1950s and was influenced by earlier modernist movements such as Bauhaus and De Stijl. Designers like Josef Müller-Brockmann emphasized rational organization, mathematical grids, and objective communication.

The style became internationally influential due to its effectiveness in public signage, editorial design, and corporate identity systems.

Core Principles

  • Grid-based layout systems
  • Sans-serif typography (e.g., Helvetica)
  • Asymmetrical composition
  • Minimal ornamentation
  • Clear visual hierarchy
