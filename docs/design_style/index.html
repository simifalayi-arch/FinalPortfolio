Swiss Style:
The Art of Precision
and Clarity

The Swiss Style, also known as the International Typographic Style, is a design movement focused on clarity, objectivity, and structure.

History

Originating in Switzerland during the 1950s, Swiss design emerged as a response to decorative excess, emphasizing rationality and order.

Core Principles

  • Grid-based layouts
  • Sans-serif typography
  • Asymmetry
  • Minimal ornamentation
