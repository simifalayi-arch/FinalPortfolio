Art Nouveau

A decorative design movement inspired by nature, flowing lines, and organic forms.

Historical Context

Art Nouveau emerged in the late 19th century as a response to industrialization and mass production. Designers sought to reintroduce craftsmanship and beauty into everyday objects.

Key Characteristics

  • Curving, sinuous lines
  • Nature-inspired motifs such as flowers and vines
  • Decorative typography
  • Emphasis on handcrafted aesthetics

Why It Matters

Art Nouveau helped bridge traditional art and modern design. Its emphasis on visual harmony and artistic expression influenced architecture, graphic design, and typography well into the modern era.
