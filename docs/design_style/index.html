Art Nouveau

Art Nouveau is a decorative design movement inspired by nature, flowing forms, and handcrafted detail.

Historical Context

Art Nouveau emerged in the late 19th century as a response to industrialization and mass production. Designers sought to restore beauty and craftsmanship to everyday objects.

Key Characteristics

  • Organic, flowing “whiplash” lines
  • Nature-inspired motifs such as flowers and vines
  • Decorative typography and illustration
  • Emphasis on craftsmanship and artistic unity

Why It Matters

Art Nouveau helped bridge traditional art and modern design by integrating aesthetics into architecture, graphic design, typography, and everyday objects. Its influence can still be seen in contemporary branding and visual storytelling.
